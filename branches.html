Indian Marvels

+

Group Discussion Summary

In our discussion, we examined the challenges faced by major Indian heritage sites like the Taj Mahal, Hawa Mahal, Qutub Minar, and the Ajanta and Ellora Caves, including environmental pollution, structural deterioration, and human-induced damage. We highlighted government actions such as the creation of protective zones, conservation by the ASI, virtual tourism promotion, and public awareness efforts. We also emphasized the critical role of NGOs and local communities in supporting preservation initiatives. A collaborative approach is essential to safeguard India's cultural legacy for future generations.

Indian Turbans

+

Group Discussion Summary

In the discussion, we explored how turbans vary across India, from the Sikh Dastar symbolizing faith to colorful Rajasthani Safas, Maharashtrian Phetas, and Mysore Petas. We covered their deep historical roots, evolving modern relevance, and challenges like stereotypes and discrimination, especially for Sikhs post-9/11. Turbans symbolize honor, identity, and protection but can face misunderstandings in urban and global settings. The group encouraged balanced participation and reflected on whether the younger generation values the turban as much as earlier ones.

Flip Book

This flip book explores the rich tradition of turbans across India, highlighting their historical origins, cultural significance, and regional variations. From the royal Pagris of Rajasthan to the sacred Dastar of Punjab, each turban style tells a story of identity, pride, and tradition. The book also examines the methods of tying turbans, their symbolism in different communities, and their evolving role in contemporary society. By documenting these diverse headgears, the flipbook aims to preserve and celebrate an important aspect of India's vibrant heritage for future generations.

Satvik Aahar

+

Group Discussion Summary

Our team delved deep into the timeless wisdom of Satvik Aahaar, uncovering its Ayurvedic roots and its powerful impact on mental clarity, physical vitality, and spiritual harmony. We exchanged personal stories, reflected on its role in our busy modern lives, and brainstormed ways to seamlessly blend these ancient practices with contemporary dietary habits. The discussion sparked a shared realization: Satvik Aahaar isn’t just a diet — it's a way of living consciously, with balance, gratitude, and purpose. Together, we envisioned how reviving Satvik principles could pave the way for a healthier, more mindful future.

Flip Book

Our flipbook brings the story of Satvik Aahaar to life — tracing its sacred journey from ancient scriptures to its vibrant role in today’s world. Through captivating visuals, bite-sized texts, and inspiring daily meal ideas, we showcase how timeless Satvik principles can be lovingly woven into modern lifestyles. Each page is a celebration of purity, balance, and the art of mindful eating, inviting readers to rediscover the true essence of nourishment.

Creative Activity

We designed an interactive mobile app called Satvik Pal — your personal companion on the journey to holistic well-being. Satvik Pal curates daily meal suggestions based on your unique body type and the rhythm of the seasons, ensuring harmony with nature. Beyond food, it offers meditation tips, mindful living practices, and Satvik lifestyle guides, making ancient wisdom beautifully accessible for the modern soul.
